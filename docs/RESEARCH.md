## 1. Mental Health & Depression

### Pooled Prevalence Among Sex Workers (Systematic Review)

| Condition | Prevalence | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|
| **Depression** | **41.8%** | 35.8% – 48.0% |
| **Anxiety** | **21.0%** | 4.8% – 58.4% |
| **PTSD** | **19.7%** | 3.2% – 64.6% |
| **Psychological distress** | **40.8%** | 20.7% – 64.4% |

> **Source:** Beattie TS, Smilenova B, Krishnaratne S, Myles A. "Mental health problems among female sex workers in low- and middle-income countries: A systematic review and meta-analysis." *PLOS Medicine*, 2020.
> [https://journals.plos.org/plosmedicine/article?id=10.1371/journal.pmed.1003297](https://journals.plos.org/plosmedicine/article?id=10.1371/journal.pmed.1003297)
>
> 68 papers, 56 unique studies, 24,940 participants across 26 LMICs. Average age 28.9 years; 46% had primary education or less.

### Key Associations Found

- **Violence ↔ Depression**: Significant positive correlation
- **Violence ↔ Suicidal behavior**: Significant positive correlation
- **Alcohol use ↔ Suicidal behavior**: Significant positive correlation
- **Drug use ↔ Depression**: Significant positive correlation
- **Depression ↔ Inconsistent condom use**: Significant positive correlation
- **Depression ↔ HIV**: Significant positive correlation

### Vancouver Cohort (AESHA Study)

- **42%** of 867 women sex workers had been diagnosed with depression, anxiety, or PTSD
- Among those reporting suicidality: **67.4%** had depression/anxiety/PTSD (vs. 37.2% among non-suicidal)
- Mental health issues (depression, anxiety, PTSD) → **AOR 2.19** (95% CI 1.63–2.95) for suicidality

> **Source:** Argento E, Strathdee SA, Shoveller JA, Braschel M, Shannon K. "Correlates of suicidality among a community-based cohort of women sex workers: The protective effect of social cohesion." *J Interpers Violence*, 2019; 36(19-20): 9709–9724. PMC7608559.
> [https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7608559/](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7608559/)

## 2. Suicide & Self-Harm

### Prevalence Among Sex Workers

| Metric | Rate | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Lifetime suicidality | **48%** (413 of 867 women) | Argento et al. 2019, Vancouver |
| Lifetime suicidal ideation | **46%** | Argento et al. 2019 |
| Lifetime suicide attempts | **33%** | Argento et al. 2019 |
| Recent suicidality (6 months) | **16%** at baseline; **29%** over study | Argento et al. 2019 |
| Suicide as cause of death among sex workers | **4.5%** (vs. 1.4% general population) | Potterat et al. 2004 |
| Recent suicide ideation (pooled) | **22.8%** (95% CI 13.2%–36.5%) | Beattie et al. 2020 |
| Recent suicide attempt (pooled) | **6.3%** (95% CI 3.4%–11.4%) | Beattie et al. 2020 |

### Trans Women Sex Workers

- **74%** lifetime suicidal ideation
- **64%** had attempted suicide
- Among 573 trans women with history of sex work in California

> **Source:** Nemoto T, Boedeker B, Iwamoto M. "Social support, exposure to violence and transphobia, and correlates of depression among male-to-female transgender women with a history of sex work." *Am J Public Health*, 2011; 101(10): 1980–1988.

### Risk Factors for Suicidality (Adjusted Odds Ratios)

| Factor | AOR | 95% CI | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Physical/sexual childhood abuse | **2.99** | 1.75 – 5.10 | < 0.001 |
| Depression, anxiety, or PTSD | **2.19** | 1.63 – 2.95 | < 0.001 |
| Intimate partner violence | **2.11** | 1.60 – 2.80 | < 0.001 |
| Physical/sexual client violence | **1.82** | 1.33 – 2.50 | < 0.001 |
| Homelessness | **1.44** | 1.10 – 1.89 | 0.008 |

> 91% of suicidal sex workers reported childhood physical/sexual abuse (vs. 63% non-suicidal). Source: Argento et al. 2019.

### Protective Factor

- **Social cohesion**: For every 1 SD increase from mean → **12% decrease** in odds of suicidality (AOR 0.88, 95% CI 0.78–0.99)

## 3. Exploitation & Abuse in Webcam Studios

### Human Rights Watch Investigation — Colombia (2024)

**Study details:** 18-month investigation, 55 interviews with studio-based webcam models across Bogotá, Cali, Medellín, and Palmira. Published December 9, 2024.

#### Key Findings

| Finding | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Terms of service** | 49 of 50 models had **never seen or signed** the ToS for any platform |
| **Income** | Workers receive as little as **10%** of the income their performance generates |
| **Revenue split** | Platforms retain 50–65%; studios keep ~60% of remainder |
| **Shift length** | Models pressured to stream **8+ hours** without breaks; some up to **18–24 hours** |
| **Coercion** | Studio managers accepted client requests for sex acts **on the model's behalf**, before consent |
| **Sexual assault** | Models reported being sexually assaulted on camera by managers in response to client requests |
| **Child exploitation** | 7 models reported working in webcam studios **before age 18** (some from age 13) |
| **Recycled accounts** | Studios use already-verified accounts of former models to bypass age verification for minors |
| **Sanitary conditions** | Studios infested with cockroaches, bedbugs; bodily fluids, used condoms, and piles of hair found in work rooms |
| **Fines** | Models fined for taking breaks or pausing streams |

#### Platforms Investigated
- **BongaCams**
- **Chaturbate**
- **LiveJasmin**
- **Stripchat**

> *"One thing in this job is that you always have to pretend to be happy."*
> — Webcam model quoted in the report

> *"If it's happening in your supply chain, it's your responsibility."*
> — Erin Kilbride, author, Human Rights Watch

> **Source:** Kilbride E. "'I Learned How to Say No': Labor Abuses & Sexual Exploitation in Colombian Webcam Studios." Human Rights Watch, December 9, 2024.
> [https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/09/i-learned-how-say-no/labor-abuses-sexual-exploitation-colombian-webcam-studios](https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/09/i-learned-how-say-no/labor-abuses-sexual-exploitation-colombian-webcam-studios)

> **ICIJ reporting:** [https://www.icij.org/news/2024/12/as-billion-dollar-webcam-industry-booms-models-suffer-at-hands-of-colombian-studios/](https://www.icij.org/news/2024/12/as-billion-dollar-webcam-industry-booms-models-suffer-at-hands-of-colombian-studios/)

## 4. Human Trafficking & Cybersex Trafficking

### Cybersex Trafficking Overview

- **Definition:** A cybercrime involving sex trafficking and live-streaming of coerced sexual acts/rape on webcam
- Victims transported to "**cybersex dens**" equipped with webcams; forced to perform for live remote consumers
- Described as a **billion-dollar illicit industry**; no comprehensive data on global magnitude exists
- Victims are predominantly **women and children**; abducted, threatened, deceived, or drugged
- Gang rape on webcam has been documented
- Victims denied food, deprived of sleep, forced to perform when sick
- Facilitated by: encrypted communications, dark web, social media, Skype, cryptocurrencies

#### Key Regions

| Region | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Philippines** | Major global hub for cybersex trafficking |
| **South Korea** | Nth room case — at least 74 victims including 16 minors |
| **Colombia** | Rapidly growing webcam industry with documented studio abuses |
| **Netherlands** | Cases documented |
| **Romania** | Cases documented |

> **Source:** Wikipedia. "Cybersex trafficking." Accessed 2025.
> [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cybersex_trafficking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cybersex_trafficking)

### OnlyFans and Human Trafficking

- **187.9 million users** and **2.1 million content creators** (as of 2021)
- OnlyFans takes a **20% cut** of all creator revenue
- Age verification is **easily bypassed** — BBC investigation found multiple cases of minors
- In 2019, ~12 missing children were linked to OnlyFans content; by 2020, that number **nearly tripled** (NCMEC)
- An investigator found **10 CSAM images** sourced from OnlyFans within **one hour** on other platforms — youngest victim: **~5 years old**
- "Romeo pimp" / "boyfriending" model used to lure teenagers onto the platform
- "E-pimps" masquerading as "management" control accounts, set prices, and take earnings

#### Confirmed Trafficking Cases Involving OnlyFans

- **Florida:** 16-year-old girl exploited by a couple; identified by a friend
- **Georgia:** Woman's footage sold without consent by a 38-year-old man
- **Texas:** Couple used OnlyFans content to target 30 junior high school boys via Snapchat
- **New York:** Girl trafficked starting at age 13; assaulted and filmed for OnlyFans for 2 years
- **Florida:** Woman severely beaten; forced to create OnlyFans account with abuser keeping all money
- **Andrew Tate case:** Women coerced into producing OnlyFans content; fined for showing emotional distress

> **Source:** The Exodus Road. "The Role of OnlyFans in Human Trafficking." February 12, 2024.
> [https://theexodusroad.com/the-role-of-onlyfans-in-human-trafficking/](https://theexodusroad.com/the-role-of-onlyfans-in-human-trafficking/)

## 5. Financial Exploitation & Platform Economics

### Revenue Structure

| Entity | Take |
|---|---|
| Webcam platforms (BongaCams, Chaturbate, etc.) | **50–65%** of model's earnings |
| Studios | **~60%** of what's left after platform cut |
| **Model (actual performer)** | **As little as 10%** of total revenue generated |
| OnlyFans | **20%** of creator revenue |

### Platform Profits

| Platform / Owner | Profit | Period |
|---|---|---|
| **Stripchat** (Cyprus-based) | **$100M+** gross profits | 2022 |
| **OnlyFans** (Leonid Radvinsky) | **$1 billion** in dividends | 2021–2023 |
| **LiveJasmin** (Gyorgy Gattyan) | Billionaire status via LiveJasmin fortune | Forbes Billionaires List |

### Creator Reality

- **Average OnlyFans creator earns ~$180/month**
- **73%** of OnlyFans total revenue comes from just the **top 10%** of creators
- The vast majority of creators earn minimal income while the platform profits enormously

> **Sources:**
> - HRW Report 2024 (revenue splits, studio economics)
> - ICIJ 2024 (platform profits: Stripchat, LiveJasmin, OnlyFans dividends)
> - Project Mona's House (average creator earnings): [https://www.projectmonashouse.com/post/onlyfans-empowering-or-exploitative](https://www.projectmonashouse.com/post/onlyfans-empowering-or-exploitative)

## 7. Digital Sexual Violence

### Srivastava et al. (2022) — US National Sample

Study of 970 sexual minority adolescents (ages 14–17):

| Metric | Rate |
|---|---|
| Threatened with nonconsensual posting of explicit media | **9.1%** |
| Had media posted without consent | **6.5%** |
| Overall suicidal ideation | **39.7%** |
| Suicide plan | **23.2%** |
| Suicide attempt | **10.8%** |
| Self-harm | **43.4%** |

#### Association: Threat to Post Explicit Media →

| Outcome | Odds Ratio |
|---|---|
| Suicide ideation | **OR 1.88** |
| Suicide plan | **OR 2.12** |
| **Suicide attempt** | **OR 3.56** |
| Self-harm | **OR 1.96** |

#### Association: Nonconsensual Posting →

| Outcome | Odds Ratio |
|---|---|
| Suicide ideation | **OR 1.82** |
| **Suicide attempt** | **OR 2.20** |

> **Source:** Srivastava A, et al. "Digital Sexual Violence and Suicide Risk." *J Interpers Violence*, 2022. PMC9850373.
> [https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9850373/](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9850373/)
